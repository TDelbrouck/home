# Thorsten Delbrouck
  
Grown up in 8-bit land! 🤖

- Group CSO at Giesecke+Devrient
- Chairman ISF
  
---  
  
As Head of Security at Giesecke+Devrient, a global security technology group, I’m responsible for the full spectrum of security topics – from information and IT security to cyber security. I also serve as Chair of the Supervisory Board of the Information Security Forum (ISF) in London and am a member of the Federation of German Industries (BDI) working group on cyber security and economic protection.

Before my current role, I built and led the security consulting practice of a German consulting firm. Earlier in my career, I served as CISO at Infineon Technologies, a German semiconductor manufacturer.

I grew up in the 8‑bit era of the early 1980s and have been “online” since the late part of that decade, starting with mailbox networks like FidoNet before moving on to the early Internet. My academic background is in Engineering Management, and in the late 1990s I authored and co‑authored several books on Internet and online technologies.

Today, my main interests are modern management approaches for information security teams and designing forward‑looking strategies for people, processes, and technology in global environments. I’m particularly focused on managing the transition from traditional on‑premises infrastructures to modern cloud architectures, and on understanding what this means for different parts of a business—especially strategy, finance, M&A, legal, compliance, and risk management.

Beyond my day‑to‑day work, I enjoy discussing the broader societal and economic impact of security with politicians, lawmakers, and academics, with the goal of contributing to an informed and ongoing public dialogue about complex security topics.
